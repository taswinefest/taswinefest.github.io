Frequently Asked Questions

  • Getting There

    The Tasmanian Wine Festival is held at the Royal Tasmanian Botanical Gardens.

    Entry to the event will be via the top main gate (Lower Domain Road). All other gates will be closed. No entry through lower gate (Domain Highway). There is strictly limited street parking available in the proximity of the Gardens. Guests can be dropped off at the top entrance to the Gardens, but please note that there is no standing.

    Seek alternative and sustainable options like - carpooling, public transport, taxi's, Uber, walk, ride or be piggy backed by your friend.

  • What are the session times?

    Friday Evening: 4pm - 9pm
    Saturday Afternoon: 12pm - 4pm
    Saturday Evening: 5pm - 9pm
    Sunday Afternoon 12 - 5pm

    Tickets are available here

  • How much does it cost?

    Tickets to the Tasmanian Wine Festival are $29+booking fee if purchased prior to the event.

  • How do I purchase tickets?

    Tickets can be purchased through Oztix, and at select cellar doors in Tasmania.

  • Do I need to print my tickets?

    No, tickets purchased from Oztix can be scanned from your phone.

  • What do I get?

    • Access to 35 Tasmanian Vineyards in one location
    • Heaps of local food options
    • Live music from great local bands & artists
    • Premium reuseable wine glass

  • What can I bring?

    Picnic Rugs, low line chairs, a hat, sunscreen, warm clothing for evenings, & ID for those over 18 who wish to purchase alcohol. There will be some long tables and chairs set up but we cannot guarantee seats for everyone, so please take advantage of the garden location and make sure to bring your rug

  • What can't I bring?

    • Glassware
    • Alcohol
    • Animals
    • Dangerous goods or illegal substances
    • Food - We have a wide range of food options available at the event
    • We ask that you are patient upon entry while bag checks are undertaken

  • What COVID-19 Safety Measures are in place?

    The Tasmanian Wine Festival meets all current regulatory requirements to ensure that this event is COVIDSafe. A COVID-19 Safety Plan has been implemented to ensure that the festival can proceed safely.

  • Accessibility

    The Tasmanian Wine Festival is committed to making the festival accessible for everyone. Whilst we endeavour to be all inclusive there may be some areas of the botanical gardens that may not be fully accessible.

    See our contact page for more how to reach us!
