FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

    When & Where

  • Where is the event?

    The Tasmanian Wine Festival is held at the Royal Tasmanian Botanical Gardens in Hobart, Tasmania.

    Access to the event is via the main gates on Lower Domain Road. There is no entry or parking at the bottom carpark on the Domain Highway.

  • When is the event?

    The Tasmanian Wine Festival is held annually in summer. The event is returning on Feb 7 & 8, 2026.

    The event has three sessions to choose from:

    • Saturday Afternoon - 12.00pm - 4.00pm
    • Saturday Evening - 5.30pm - 10.00pm
    • Sunday Afternoon - 12.00pm - 5.00pm

  • How do I get there?

    Entry to the event is through the Main Gates of the Botanical Gardens only, which is located on Lower Domain Road.

    There is limited parking available on Lower Domain Road (near the Main Entrance of the Botanical Gardens), as well as a few car parks surrounding the Gardens. We encourage patrons to get dropped off or use public transportation including Taxis and rideshare vehicles.

    The bottom carpark on the Domain Highway is closed off for the public. There is no parking or entry via this gate.

    • Tickets

  • How do I purchase tickets?

    Tickets can be purchased online through Oztix, our authorised ticket provider.

    Tickets should only purchased through the authorised ticket provider. Tickets brought from any other platform cannot be guaranteed as legitimate.

    Patrons with fraudulent tickets will not be admitted.

  • Is this an accessible event?

    The Tasmanian Wine Festival strives to be a fully inclusive and accessible event, however there are some limitations due to the nature of the venue we operate in.

    We encourage all patrons with accessibility requirements to contact the festival team prior to the event: info@taswinefest.com.au

  • Is this an all ages event?

    The Tasmanian Wine Festival operates sessions that are both Licensed All Ages and 18+.

    • The Saturday Afternoon Session is Licensed All Ages
    • The Saturday Evening Session is 18+
    • The Sunday Session is Licensed All Ages


    Patrons under the age of 18 must be accompanied by a parent or legal guardian above the age of 25.

    Children aged between 5-17 years must hold a ticket to gain entry. Under 5s do not require a ticket.

  • Do you accept companion cards?

    Yes. The Tasmanian Wine Festival accepts valid Companion Cards. Companion Card holders are required to contact the festival prior to the event: info@taswinefest.com.au

    Companions must arrive with the ticket holder they are with.

  • What can I bring to the event?

    What can you bring:
    • Low lying chairs - for use at the rear of the stage viewing area only. Festival staff may request chairs be moved if they impede the view of others
    • Empty Reuseable Water Bottles. There are free water refill stations available
    • Sun screen, a hat and warm clothing for the evening

    What can't you bring:
    • Food & Alcohol. This is a strictly no BYO event
    • Animals, with the exception of assistance animals
    • Glassware. All ticket holders receive a tumbler on entry
    • Weapons, fireworks or illicit substances
    • Professional film equipment
    • Speakers
    • Marketing materials of any kind

    This list is not extensive. A full list will be sent to ticket holders prior to the event.

  • Are there wine tastings?

    Most vineyards at the Tasmanian Wine Festival offer wine tastings to patrons. Tastings can be redeemed by purchasing Tasting Tokens that are available on the day, or included with certain ticket types.

    Like what you tried? Make sure to purchase a glass or bottle to support Tasmanian wine producers & vineyards!

    There are no free tastings at the Tasmanian Wine Festival

  • Are there beverages available other than wine?

    The Tasmanian Wine Festival has a wide variety of beverages available from craft beers, ciders, spirits and summer cocktails. This is in addition to a wide range of non alcoholic options.

  • What payment types are accepted?

    All vendors at the Tasmanian Wine Festival accept card payments, with some vendors accepting cash.

    Each vendor decides whether they accept cash payments. All festival bars accept both cash and card payments.

    It is recommended that all patrons bring cash and a physical card to the event. Mobile reception can be limited within the venue.

    • Reserved Tables

  • What is a Reserved Table?

    Reserved Tables at the Tasmanian Wine Festival offer groups a guaranteed reserved table at their chosen event session.

    Reserved Tables are available for groups of up to six people.

  • Do I need an event ticket if I have a Reserved Table?

    Reserved Tables do not provide access to the event.

    Each person sitting at a reserved table requires their own event ticket.

  • Where will my table be located?

    When you arrive at the event, please speak to our friendly staff who can provide directions.

  • How do I reserve a table?

    Reserved Tables can be booked through Oztix, our only authorised ticket provider.

    Reserved Tables sold out in 2025, so make sure to book yours while stocks last.
